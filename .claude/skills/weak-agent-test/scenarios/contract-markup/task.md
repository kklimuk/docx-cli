# I need our changes marked up on this contract before we sign

I run **Mercer Design Studio**, and we just got the contract draft (`contract.docx`)
from **Nimbus Freight Systems** for a project we're about to start. I finally read the
whole thing and honestly it's pretty outrageous — almost every clause is written to
protect them and dump all the risk on us. I'm not signing it as it stands.

Can you mark it up and get it ready to send back to them? Track the changes so they can
see exactly what we touched, and leave notes on the spots where we're pushing back, so
it's clear what we're objecting to and why.

## What I can't accept — these have to change

- The **non-compete** is insane — five years, the entire world, any
  industry, and a $50,000 penalty every time _they_ decide we crossed a line. No way I
  can agree to that. It needs to come out, or be cut down to something small and sane.
- The **liability cap** lets _them_ off the hook at a hundred dollars while
  _we'd_ be on the hook for everything. That's completely backwards — it has to be fair
  in both directions.
- The **payment terms** say "Net 90, at the Company's sole discretion." I'm
  not floating their bills for three months and hoping they feel like paying. We need to
  get paid on a normal, dependable schedule.
- The **indemnification** makes _us_ cover _them_ even when the problem is
  **their own fault**. I'm not signing up to pay for their negligence.

## What bugs me — flag these so we can talk it through

I'm not necessarily killing these outright, but I want my objections on the record so we
can discuss them:

- The **IP section** looks like it grabs not just the project work but things
  I do on my own time and stuff I made before we ever met. That can't be right.
- The **personal guarantee** would put _me personally_ on the hook for
  everything, not just the studio. Flag it.
- **Dispute resolution** — arbitration in front of an arbitrator _they_
  pick, with us paying all the fees up front. That's not a fair fight.
- Section 7 has us **waiving our right to our own lawyer**. Seriously?
- Section 13 lets them **change the contract later just by posting a new version to a
  web page**. Flag that one too.

## One more thing

Section 9 is also a dense, cramped wall of text — while you're in there, make it easier
to actually read. It's the clause I most want them to re-read carefully.

And a small house-style fix: in the intellectual-property section, the sub-points are
labelled a, b, c, d. We always use small Roman numerals (i, ii, iii) for sub-clauses in
our contracts — please switch those over to match.

## What "done" looks like

It comes back marked up and ready to send: our changes to the unacceptable terms are
visible as tracked edits (they'll see exactly what we struck or rewrote), my objections
show up as notes pinned to the clauses I flagged, section 9 is legible, and the IP
sub-points read as i, ii, iii. Nothing is changed silently — they can see and respond to
every edit and every note.
