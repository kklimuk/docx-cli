# Legal review: redlining + commenting

## Task

Act as the **Contractor's** outside counsel reviewing this Independent Contractor
Agreement (`contract.docx`). It is heavily one-sided in the Company's favor. With
tracked changes ON, **redline** the most one-sided terms (soften or strike them as
tracked edits/deletions), **reformat** the dense §9 clause for readability (a tracked
line-spacing change), and leave **anchored comments** flagging the clauses the
Contractor must push back on. Edit the file in place.

## Resolution criteria

Tracked changes are enabled and at least three egregious terms are revised as
**tracked** edits or deletions (visible redlines, not silent changes) — e.g. the
five-year worldwide non-compete, the $100 liability cap, Net-90 payment, or the
indemnity for the Company's own negligence. **At least one tracked paragraph-formatting
change** — §9 set to 1.5 line spacing (and/or space-after) — appears as a `pPrChange`
in `docx track-changes list` (a tracked revision, not a silent reformat). At least
four **anchored comments** sit on the relevant clauses (e.g. the IP over-assignment,
the personal guarantee, the Company-selected arbitration, the waiver of counsel).
`docx track-changes list` shows the redlines + the pPrChange and `docx comments list`
shows the comments anchored to the right text.
